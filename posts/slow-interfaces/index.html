Slow interfaces

Designing friction that keeps the reader grounded.

designinterfaceeditorial

The fastest interface is not always the most memorable. A little resistance can create intention: pauses between sections, spacious typography, and moments that invite the reader to breathe.

The quiet layer

When everything shouts, attention fractures. This is why I keep returning to white space, broad margins, and soft transitions. They are small signals that say, “read slowly.”

Speed is an affordance. Slowness is a ritual.

A checklist

  • Give the hero space.
  • Use fewer weights.
  • Let the page settle before it scrolls.

The goal is not to make the site inert, but to make it feel deliberate.
